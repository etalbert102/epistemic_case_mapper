# LHC Argument Evolution Timeline

Purpose: preserve how the LHC safety argument evolved over time.

## Timeline

- Earlier CERN safety work: safety analysis predates the 2008 LHC startup debate and provides background for later LSAG review.
- 2008 LSAG review: formal institutional safety assessment updates prior work and addresses microscopic black holes, strangelets, vacuum bubbles, and magnetic monopoles.
- 2008 Giddings-Mangano analysis: technical astrophysical argument analyzes hypothetical stable TeV-scale black holes using Earth, white-dwarf, and neutron-star constraints.
- 2008 SPC review: independent CERN Scientific Policy Committee panel reviews LSAG documents and endorses conclusions while noting scope caveats.
- 2008 Plaga critique: metastable black-hole risk argument challenges whether safety analyses fully close all scenarios.
- 2008 Giddings-Mangano response: direct rebuttal argues the critique relies on inconsistent assumptions.
- 2009 Johnson legal analysis: reframes the controversy as a legal/governance problem around injunctions and catastrophic risk.
- 2010/2011 CMS search: later empirical searches find no microscopic black-hole signatures in tested model ranges.
- Current CERN public pages: communicate the settled public-facing view and explain rapid disintegration/detectability in accessible language.

## Decision-Space Implication

The LHC case is not just "black holes are safe." It has separable layers:

- physical possibility,
- stability/accretion assumptions,
- astrophysical constraints,
- institutional review,
- public communication,
- legal/public-risk governance,
- later empirical searches.
