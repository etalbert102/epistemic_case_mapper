# Debarre and Worobey Reply Notes

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.08040
Authors: Florence Debarre and Michael Worobey
Date: 2024-05-13
Retrieved: 2026-06-27

Debarre and Worobey reply to Weissman's claim about proximity ascertainment bias. They say his conclusion rests on a flawed premise and that there is no internal evidence of major bias.

They argue that the observed pattern can be explained if infection locations were not limited to residential neighborhoods and if stochasticity is considered, without requiring major ascertainment bias.

Use in worked region: methodological challenge to one lab-leak-favoring interpretation of early case geography.
