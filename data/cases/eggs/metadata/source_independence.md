# Eggs Source Independence Notes

Purpose: prevent correlated evidence from being counted as independent.

## Independence Clusters

### Guideline And Communication Cluster

- `dga_2020_2025_pmc_summary`
- `aha_2019_dietary_cholesterol_pubmed`
- `aha_2023_dietary_cholesterol_news`
- `nnr_2023_eggs_scoping_review`

Risk: these sources may summarize overlapping evidence bases or translate evidence into policy guidance. Do not count them as independent primary evidence.

### Cohort And Meta-Analysis Cluster

- `bmj_2020_egg_consumption_cvd`
- `bmj_2013_egg_consumption_chd_stroke`
- `jama_2019_dietary_cholesterol_eggs`
- `ma_2021_egg_cvd_dose_response`

Risk: cohort studies and meta-analyses may reuse overlapping cohorts or outcome definitions. Count them as partially correlated unless the worked-region map identifies distinct underlying cohorts.

### Biomarker / RCT Cluster

- `li_2020_egg_cholesterol_rct_meta`

Risk: more internally controlled than observational studies, but endpoints are biomarkers rather than long-term clinical outcomes.

### Overview And Narrative Cluster

- `huang_2020_egg_health_outcomes_evidence_mapping`
- `eggs_dietary_cholesterol_cvd_review`
- `dietary_cholesterol_lack_evidence_cvd_review`

Risk: useful for mapping the debate, but less independent if they synthesize the same underlying studies.

## Cruxes For Worked Region

- How much weight should clinical outcome cohorts receive relative to short-duration RCT lipid changes?
- Which meta-analytic findings are independent rather than re-statements of the same cohort base?
- When guideline statements differ from study-level findings, is the difference due to risk management, substitution context, or evidence uncertainty?
