# Eggs Guideline And Evidence Evolution Timeline

Purpose: help future worked-region agents preserve how the eggs question changed over time instead of collapsing the case into a single timeless recommendation.

## Timeline

- Pre-2015 pattern: dietary cholesterol and eggs were commonly treated as direct cardiovascular-risk concerns.
- 2013: BMJ dose-response meta-analysis reported cohort evidence that complicated a simple egg-risk narrative, while still leaving subgroup questions such as diabetes.
- 2019: JAMA pooled cohort analysis reported associations between dietary cholesterol or egg consumption and incident CVD/mortality, strengthening the cautionary side of the evidence map.
- 2019: AHA dietary cholesterol science advisory framed cholesterol risk through broader dietary patterns and cardiovascular risk management.
- 2020: Dietary Guidelines for Americans emphasized healthy dietary patterns and protein-food variety, treating eggs as one protein-food source rather than only a cholesterol object.
- 2020: BMJ cohorts/meta-analysis reported no clear overall association in large cohorts and updated meta-analysis, reinforcing the importance of study design and adjustment choices.
- 2020: RCT lipid meta-analysis clarified biomarker effects, especially LDL-C/HDL-C framing, but did not directly settle long-term clinical outcomes.
- 2023: Nordic Nutrition Recommendations scoping review summarized egg evidence for guideline use and highlighted outcome-specific uncertainty.

## Decision-Space Implication

The live question is not "Are eggs good or bad?" It is:

- for whom,
- replacing what,
- at what intake level,
- against which endpoint,
- under which evidential standard.
