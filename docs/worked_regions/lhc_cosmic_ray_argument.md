## Why This Region Matters

This region is a compact stress test for decision-space preservation. A normal synthesis can correctly report that the LHC was judged safe, while hiding the conditions that make the conclusion robust: natural exposure analogies, low-velocity trapping differences, compact-star observations, worst-case assumptions, and adversarial critique/response.

## Expected Cruxes

- Whether cosmic-ray collisions are sufficiently analogous to LHC collisions after accounting for low-velocity products and trapping.
- Whether stable or metastable microscopic black holes require suppressing Hawking radiation while retaining other quantum effects.
- Whether white-dwarf observations are enough for the LHC energy range, and where neutron-star arguments add broader but more assumption-dependent support.
- Whether Plaga's scenario is a physically coherent gap in GM's bounds or rests on inconsistent assumptions.

## What Ordinary Synthesis May Flatten

- The cosmic-ray argument as a simple analogy, losing the velocity/trapping caveat.
- Compact-star evidence as a single proof, losing the white-dwarf/neutron-star split.
- Hawking radiation as either settled or irrelevant, losing its role in an assumption stack.
- The Plaga critique as merely "rejected", losing the scenario distinction and response rationale.
- The LHC-energy boundary and the need for care when extrapolating to future higher-energy colliders.
