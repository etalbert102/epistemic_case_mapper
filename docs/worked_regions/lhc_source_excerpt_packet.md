# LHC Source Excerpt Packet

Purpose: help a Codex goal run build the LHC worked region without rereading the full source corpus from scratch. This packet is an orientation layer, not a substitute for checking the source text before writing a claim.

Worked region: `lhc_cosmic_ray_argument`

Primary question: does the cosmic-ray safety argument, including compact-star variants and critiques, rule out decision-relevant LHC microscopic-black-hole risk?

## High-Value Source Spans

### `lsag_2008_safety_review`

- Lines 119-138: frames the cosmic-ray exposure argument and states that if LHC microscopic black holes could be dangerous, analogous cosmic-ray production plus astronomical-body survival should already reveal the danger.
- Lines 175-183: estimates Earth exposure to cosmic rays at or above LHC-equivalent energy and states the planet still exists.
- Lines 193-207: extends the exposure argument to the Sun, the Milky Way, and the visible Universe.
- Lines 292-298: flags the key velocity-frame caveat: LHC products tend to have low velocities, while cosmic-ray products tend to have high velocities.
- Lines 315-327: summarizes the Hawking-radiation expectation while acknowledging lack of direct experimental detection.
- Lines 329-350: gives a separate quantum-mechanical decay argument and states that stable microscopic black holes would require suppressing basic quantum or relativistic principles.

Candidate map use:

- Exposure analogy.
- Velocity caveat.
- Hawking-radiation assumption.
- Independent decay argument.
- Stable-black-hole contingency.

### `spc_2008_lsag_review`

- Lines 43-65: independent review endorses the LSAG cosmic-ray comparison as the basis for later safety proofs.
- Lines 67-72: states the comparison relies on special relativity and notes relevant experimental support.
- Lines 101-128: decomposes the black-hole risk scenario into unlikely assumptions and says the GM paper uses conservative worst-case treatment plus astronomical observations.
- Lines 130-137: distinguishes the strength of the white-dwarf argument for LHC energies from the neutron-star argument for higher energies.
- Lines 167-178: says LSAG adds safety layers by replacing some theoretical concepts with observational data on cosmic rays and astronomical bodies.

Candidate map use:

- Independent endorsement.
- Assumption stack.
- White-dwarf vs neutron-star scope.
- LHC-only boundary.
- Observational-data upgrade over theory-only assurance.

### `giddings_mangano_2008_stable_black_holes`

- Lines 2402-2411: argues that scenarios dangerous to Earth would imply faster disruption of white dwarfs, contrary to observed lifetimes.
- Lines 2415-2460: breaks conclusions by dimensionality, including white-dwarf bounds for D <= 7 and neutron-star bounds for D >= 8.
- Lines 2465-2477: lists the unlikely conditions required before the stable-black-hole bounds become relevant.
- Lines 2478-2508: summarizes added safety layers: crossover scale, white-dwarf contradiction, neutron-star contradiction under cosmic-ray assumptions.
- Lines 3600-3641: analyzes LHC trapping and notes cosmic-ray black holes are highly relativistic, while some LHC black holes may slow enough for capture.
- Lines 3710-3716: estimates trapped LHC black holes and links mass ranges to compact-star exclusions.
- Lines 3866-3891: considers black holes produced in interstellar medium before reaching stars and says some white dwarfs would still be destroyed under the dangerous scenarios.

Candidate map use:

- Compact-star contradiction.
- Dimensional split.
- Trapping caveat.
- Worst-case assumption ladder.
- Cosmic-ray composition and neutrino caveats.

### `plaga_2008_metastable_black_holes`

- Lines 18-31: states the critique's core claim that a metastable scenario might evade GM/Koch exclusions and remain risky.
- Lines 94-107: summarizes GM's scenario-2 setup and the use of white-dwarf observations to exclude dangerous parameters.
- Lines 421-430: argues that safety intuitions about slow accretion and compact-star catastrophe are insufficient guarantees under the critique's scenario.
- Lines 439-471: claims a gap in GM's white-dwarf stopping argument because semiclassical assumptions may not cover quantum-gravity-regime black holes.
- Lines 481-498: summarizes concern that Eddington-limited accretion with Hawking radiation may not be excluded by GM/Koch.
- Lines 563-606: replies to GM's comment by saying GM criticized a scenario Plaga did not intend and that GM scenario-2 bounds do not apply to the proposed scenario-3 accretion.

Candidate map use:

- Adversarial critique.
- Scenario distinction.
- Semiclassical-assumption gap.
- Eddington-limited accretion claim.
- Disagreement over what GM's bounds actually cover.

## Likely Cruxes

- Whether cosmic-ray collisions are sufficiently analogous to LHC collisions once product velocity and trapping differ.
- Whether Hawking-radiation failure can be separated from other quantum effects needed for neutralization and decay.
- Whether white-dwarf survival is a strong enough observational bound for the LHC energy range.
- Whether neutron-star constraints require uncertain cosmic-ray composition or neutrino assumptions.
- Whether Plaga's metastable/Eddington-limited scenario is physically coherent or outside GM's original bound.

## Common Flat-Synthesis Losses To Test

- Treating the cosmic-ray argument as a single simple analogy and omitting the low-velocity LHC trapping caveat.
- Saying "compact stars prove safety" without preserving the white-dwarf/neutron-star split.
- Collapsing Plaga's critique into "rejected" without preserving the specific scenario distinction.
- Presenting Hawking radiation as either settled or irrelevant, rather than a condition in an assumption stack.
- Omitting the LHC-energy boundary and possible future-collider caveat.
