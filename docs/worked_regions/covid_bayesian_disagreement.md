## Why This Region Matters

COVID origins is the contest's adversarial stress test. A normal synthesis can say "judges and superforecasters leaned zoonotic, while Rootclaim, Levin, and Weissman favor lab leak or critique zoonotic subclaims." That is not enough. The reviewer needs to know which disagreements are about:

- process and debate format,
- Bayesian decomposition and priors,
- early-case geography and ascertainment bias,
- phylogenetic/multiple-introduction reasoning,
- aggregate probability versus persistent minority disagreement,
- what would update the assessment.

## Expected Cruxes

- Whether Huanan-market geography is evidence for an origin site or can be explained by ascertainment and infection-location assumptions.
- Whether formal Bayesian decompositions are robust or driven by correlated components and contestable priors.
- Whether later critiques of subarguments update the whole origins assessment or only narrow pieces of it.

## What Ordinary Synthesis May Flatten

- Judge outcome versus participant postmortem.
- Aggregate probability versus minority forecast distribution.
- Market geography versus market-origin inference.
- Single-introduction phylogeny critique versus full lab-leak conclusion.
- "Bayesian analysis" as a shared method versus incompatible decomposition choices.
