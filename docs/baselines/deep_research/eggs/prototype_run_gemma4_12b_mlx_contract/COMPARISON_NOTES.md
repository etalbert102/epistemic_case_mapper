# Briefing-Contract Comparison Notes

Question: For generally healthy adults, should eggs be treated as meaningfully harmful, neutral, or beneficial in dietary advice, especially with respect to cardiovascular risk?

## Result

The briefing-contract run is the strongest prototype output so far. It retains the map advantages while closing the biggest prose failures from the earlier runs.

Deep Research still wins on richness and natural synthesis, but the gap is narrower: the prototype now gives a more calibrated bottom line, keeps counterevidence in the right section, and avoids obvious overstatement language.

## Improvements Over Section-Only Run

- The bottom line now says moderate egg consumption is `not associated with cardiovascular disease risk overall` rather than `neutral to potentially beneficial`.
- Decision implications avoid `safe` / `safely` language.
- The strongest counterposition is visible in the bottom line and crux table.
- Concern evidence from Spence/Zhong remains under `Conflicting Evidence`, not `Main Support`.
- The final output avoids `Claim A`, `Claim B`, `mapped claim`, and `Both claims` phrasing.
- Diabetes-related evidence is framed as limited by study duration rather than as a broad safety claim.

## Remaining Weaknesses Versus Deep Research

- Deep Research remains more fluent and better at narrative synthesis.
- The conflict prose still contains some awkward relation-derived sentences.
- Subgroup treatment is still thinner than Deep Research's discussion of diabetes, high LDL/ApoB, familial hypercholesterolemia, high intake, high baseline risk, and hyper-responders.
- Quantitative detail is still limited because the reconstructed source packet is mostly PubMed abstracts.
- The prototype's audit trail is useful for inspection but still too mechanical for a polished final report.

## Updated Assessment

The generalizable contract approach is promising. It does not hardcode the eggs domain; instead, it uses generic answer-frame, scope-ledger, evidence-direction, and overstatement-lint structures. On this case, those generic controls improved prose quality while preserving the prototype's inspectability advantage.

The next improvement should target relation-to-prose rewriting: relation rationales should be compressed into clean human sentences before final rendering, especially in `Conflicting Evidence` and `Audit Trail`.
