## Evidence Roles

### Main Support

- Egg consumption is associated with a risk of cardiovascular disease, including coronary heart disease and stroke. (Eggs A 2023)
- Studies have not generally supported an association between dietary cholesterol and cardiovascular risk. (Here's The Latest On Dietary Cholesterol And How It Fits In With A Healthy Diet)
- Moderate egg consumption (up to one egg per day) is not associated with cardiovascular disease risk overall. (Egg Consumption And Risk Of Cardiovascular Disease: Three Large Prospective US Cohort Studies, Systematic Review, And Updated Meta Analysis)
- Egg consumption significantly increases LDL-c levels and the LDL-c/HDL-c ratio. (Association Between Egg Consumption And Cholesterol Concentration: A Systematic Review And Meta Analysis Of Randomized Controlled Trials)
- Egg consumption did not effectively increase HDL-c levels. (Association Between Egg Consumption And Cholesterol Concentration: A Systematic Review And Meta Analysis Of Randomized Controlled Trials)

### Conflicting Evidence

- High egg consumption was associated with a higher risk of cardiovascular disease in people with type 2 diabetes. (Egg Consumption And Risk Of Cardiovascular Disease: Three Large Prospective US Cohort Studies, Systematic Review, And Updated Meta Analysis)
- Anyone with a high LDL cholesterol level should consider reducing sources of both saturated fat and dietary cholesterol. (Here's The Latest On Dietary Cholesterol And How It Fits In With A Healthy Diet)
- Egg consumption is associated with an increased risk of ovarian cancer. (Eggs A 2023)

### Scope Limits

- The association between egg consumption and cardiovascular disease risk was found to be lower in Asian cohorts compared to US and European cohorts. (Egg Consumption And Risk Of Cardiovascular Disease: Three Large Prospective US Cohort Studies, Systematic Review, And Updated Meta Analysis)
