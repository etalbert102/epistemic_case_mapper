# Deterministic Flat Retrieval Proxy

Question: Why can randomized lipid results and observational cardiovascular outcomes point in different directions without one simply invalidating the other?

Recovered passages from the flat synthesis:

The NNR 2023 scoping review summarized the total picture as follows: RCTs suggest lipid changes with heterogeneous responses, while recent observational evidence does not strongly support harm from moderate intake up to one egg per day, especially in European populations.

Randomized trial evidence adds another layer.

Li 2020 found that higher egg consumption in healthy participants increased LDL cholesterol and the LDL-c/HDL-c ratio, though the trials measured biomarkers rather than long-term cardiovascular events and did not establish a clean dose trend.

The egg evidence is mixed but points toward a moderate, diet-pattern-based conclusion.

Instead, they emphasize healthy dietary patterns that are low in saturated fat, rich in plant foods, and generally keep dietary cholesterol low without compromising nutritional adequacy.
