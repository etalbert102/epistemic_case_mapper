# Deterministic Flat Retrieval Proxy

Question: Why do compact astronomical bodies become relevant?

Recovered passages from the flat synthesis:

White dwarfs and neutron stars have survived for long periods, so the relevant dangerous parameter ranges are treated as excluded by observation.

The LSAG review argues that the Earth, Sun, and many other astronomical bodies have been exposed to enormous numbers of such events over long timescales and still exist.

The CERN Scientific Policy Committee endorsed this reasoning, noting that the survival of these bodies is difficult to reconcile with a scenario in which LHC-scale collisions could produce objects that catastrophically destroy matter.

Even if one assumes a conservative worst case in which black holes could be stable, the Giddings-Mangano analysis argues that dangerous scenarios would also lead cosmic-ray-produced black holes to be trapped in compact astronomical objects.

The compact-star arguments are also technical and vary by assumptions about extra dimensions, black-hole production, accretion, cosmic-ray composition, and neutrinos.
