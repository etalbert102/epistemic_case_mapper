## Evidence Roles

### Main Support

- Professional Superforecasters assessed natural zoonosis as the most likely cause (74%) and a research-related accident as second most likely (25%), while noting that disagreement persisted among forecasters. (Superforecasting The Origins Of The COVID 19 Pandemic)
- The observed pattern of early cases can be explained by non-residential infection locations and stochasticity without requiring major ascertainment bias. (No Evidence Of Systematic Proximity Ascertainment Bias In Early COVID 19 Cases In Wuhan)
- This source should be used for participant postmortem and process critique, not as a neutral adjudication of the debate. (Rootclaim's COVID 19 Origins Debate Results)

### Scope Limits

- The source is a scope-setting brief for an epistemic case study, not an independent scientific authority on COVID origins. (FLF Epistemic Case Study Competition COVID Brief)
- The source provides an independent probabilistic assessment and a structure for representing disagreement. (Superforecasting The Origins Of The COVID 19 Pandemic)
- This source addresses a specific phylogenetic/multiple-introduction subissue rather than the entire origins question. (An Article In Science On COVID Origins Contains A Fundamental Error)
- The judges' probabilistic inference assigned unrealistic numbers to evidence, and Rootclaim failed to obtain ongoing judge feedback. (Rootclaim's COVID 19 Origins Debate Results)

### Method Limits

- The value of a public debate can be obscured by the risk that 'nobody accepted my challenge' rhetoric is mistaken for evidence. (Practically A Book Review: Rootclaim $100,000 Lab Leak Debate)
- Levin's NBER working paper evaluates lab leak versus zoonotic transmission using Bayesian methods and spatiotemporal/zoonotic data. (A Bayesian Assessment Of The Origins Of COVID 19 Using Spatiotemporal And Zoonotic Data)
- The 2024 Rootclaim debate involved a public dispute over epidemiology, viral genetics, Bayesian inference, and institutional analysis regarding COVID origins. (FLF Epistemic Case Study Competition COVID Brief)
- The live format of the debate advantaged debaters with more memorized detail, suggesting an offline text format for future debates. (Rootclaim's COVID 19 Origins Debate Results)
